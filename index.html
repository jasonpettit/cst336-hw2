My Mad-lib Game

Let's create an Mad-lib! Ad libitum is latin for "at one's pleasure" or "as you desire". Mad-libs follow that style by asking you to select words "as you desire" to complete an unknown story. Mad-libs were invented 1953, and were first released in 1958, by Leonard Stern and Roger Price.
(learn more about Mad-libs here on Wikipedia, or on the Mad-Libs official site.)

The inputs for Mad-libs are words that will be placed into the unknown story, where a word of that type is needed.

Grammar reminders:

  • Noun: Name of a person, place, or thing. (George Washington, summer, dog, table)
  • Adjective: Word that describes someone or something. (tall, hot, fuzzy, boring)
  • Verb: An action word. (run, fall, jump, cover)
  • Adverb: Word that tells how something is done, and often ends in -ly. (quickly, carefully, beautifully, simply)
